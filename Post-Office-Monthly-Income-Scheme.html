Details

Post Office Monthly Income Scheme (POMIS) is an investment scheme recognized and validated by the Ministry of Finance. It provides a monthly interest payout for a period of 5 years and is popular among conservative investors and senior citizens seeking steady income. Minimum deposit is ₹1,500. Interest is paid monthly by the post office.

Features of POMIS

  • Fixed monthly interest payout for the 5-year tenure.
  • No compounding on unpaid interest — interest paid monthly only.
  • Joint accounts allowed (up to 3 adults).
  • Older accounts (before 1-Dec-2011) may have bonus benefits (5%).
  • Low-risk — backed by Government of India.

POMIS interest for senior citizens often follows the prevailing scheme rate. The lock-in period is a minimum of 5 years and withdrawals before maturity attract penalties.

How to Open an Account (Offline)

  1. Open a Post Office Savings Account if you don't have one.
  2. Obtain and fill the POMIS application form (download from India Post or collect at the Post Office).
  3. Submit self-attested copies of KYC documents and carry originals for verification.
  4. Mention nominee details (name, DOB, mobile).
  5. Deposit the initial amount (min ₹1,500) via cash or cheque.

Penalty for Premature Withdrawal

  • 0–1 Year: No withdrawal allowed
  • 1–3 Years: Allowed with 2% deduction from principal
  • 3–5 Years: Allowed with 1% deduction from principal

Who is eligible?

Resident Indians (NRIs excluded). Applicants must be 18+. Guardians can open for minors aged 10+.
